Who is Jack Pilgrim?
3/12/26
Orel Finch

"Do you need anything? Water?", Jack says to me looking up from his monitor. His face is only illuminated by the glow of the monitors before him. For several hours I've sat and reviewed work in chair in the corner of his office while he crunched in the dark to finish a feature for a beta launch later in the week. "No thanks, I'm all set" I say. If anyone else had wasted my time like that I would have left immediately. But Jack is special.

Jack is a quiet man. Slender and tall with deep brown eyes. Much has been written about him in recent years. A math prodigy turned CEO who seems to only take mild satisfaction in solving some of the biggest open questions in computer science.

His latest project: a revolutionary social media platform that gives privacy back to the people.

After he was done working he showed me a demo of the platform. It's not particularly special as far as social apps go. It's UI is functional but unappetizing, but I suspect it will dramatically change the world.

Jack lives in a small studio apartment, drives a prius, and only takes a modest salary. His company is probably the only nonprofit that has to constantly fend off a stream of big money investment deals from venture capitalists.

I came away from the meeting not knowing if jack didn't understand his impact on society, or if he was the only person who could understand it.
